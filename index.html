In recent years, a new kind of sex crime has been sweeping South Korea. It is called Molka, the Korean word for secret camera. Secret camera take a myriad of photos of women undressing, going to the toilet, in changing rooms in clothing stores, gyms and swimming pools, or even in the motel. The videos are posted online on pop-up pornography sites. Every year, over 6,000 cases of molka porn are reported to the police, and 80% of the victims are women. Surprisingly, most offenders do not go to prison and feel guilty about crimes.
